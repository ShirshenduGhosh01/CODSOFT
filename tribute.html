A Tribute To Netaji

(23 January 1897 - 18 August 1945)

Tribute to Subhas Chandra Bose

Subhas Chandra Bose, also known as Netaji, was a prominent leader in the Indian independence movement against British rule. He was known for his fierce patriotism and his advocacy for complete and unconditional independence for India.
Bose's legacy as a freedom fighter continues to inspire generations of Indians.


Biography

Born on January 23, 1897, in Cuttack, Odisha, Subhas Chandra Bose was a brilliant student and a nationalist from a young age. He was strongly influenced by Swami Vivekananda's teachings and joined the Indian National Congress, quickly rising through the ranks. Dissatisfied with the Congress's approach, he later formed the Forward Bloc.

During World War II, Bose sought help from the Axis powers to free India. He led the Indian National Army (INA) and fought alongside Japanese forces against the British. Despite his controversial methods, his determination and sacrifice made him a revered figure in Indian history.

Bose is believed to have died in a plane crash on August 18, 1945, though some details of his death remain a subject of speculation and mystery.

Memories

  • Featured on Indian stamps in 1964, 1993, 1997, 2001, 2016, 2018, and 2021.
    Featured on ₹2 coins in 1996 and 1997, ₹75 coin in 2018, and ₹125 coin in 2021.
  • Japanese Prime Minister Shinzō Abe visited Netaji Bhawan in Kolkata on 23 August 2007.
    Abe received the Netaji Award in 2022 and expressed deep respect for Bose in Japan.
  • 23 January declared as Parakram Divas by the Government of India to commemorate Bose's birth anniversary.
    Trinamool Congress and All India Forward Bloc suggested the day be observed as 'Deshprem Divas.'
  • Museum on Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose and his INA inaugurated at Red Fort, New Delhi, in 2019.
    Statue of Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose inaugurated at India Gate in 2022.
  • Subhas Chandra Bose Aapda Prabandhan Puraskar for excellence in disaster management started by the Government of India in 2022.

Famous Quotes

  • "Give me blood, and I shall give you freedom!"
  • "It is blood alone that can pay the price of freedom. Give me blood and I will give you freedom."
  • "We should have but one desire today - the desire to die so that India may live."
